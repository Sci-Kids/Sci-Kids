Lesson 1: Introduction to Science

Science is the study of the world around us. It helps increase our knowledge about the world we live in so that we can survive and evolve. Scientists are people who specialize in science, they try to learn about the world by observing, describing, and experimenting on objects. There are many different branches of science, but in this lesson, we will be focusing on science in general.

Lesson 2: Identifying Non Living and Living Things

Living things are organisms that take in nutrients, grow, and reproduce. For example, a butterfly or a sunflower. Non-living things are inanimate objects that do not grow, take in nutrients, or reproduce. For example, a rock or a toy. They both occupy space in this world and have a certain weight to them.

Lesson 3: Parts of a Plant

The basic parts of most plants are roots, stems, leaves, flowers, and seeds.

  • The roots are located underground, and they take nutrients so that the plant can be healthy and survive.
  • The stem maintains the plant firm and lets the nutrients flow throughout the whole plant.
  • The leaves prepare the food for the plant.
  • The flower makes seeds so that it can reproduce itself.
  • Lastly the seeds are the reproductive part of a plant.
